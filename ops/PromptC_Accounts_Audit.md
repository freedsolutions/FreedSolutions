# Prompt C — Accounts & Subscriptions Audit

> **When to run:** After Prompt B is complete. This is the long tail — finding every account tied to the old Gmail.
> **Critical:** Pay special attention to 2FA recovery emails and any API keys registered under the old Gmail. These are the easiest to miss and hardest to recover from.

---

## Paste this into a fresh Claude chat:

```
I have migrated from freedsolutions@gmail.com to
adam@freedsolutions.com. My tools and professional assets have
been updated. Now I need to systematically find and update every
other account, subscription, or login tied to the old Gmail.

CONTEXT:
  - I am a freelance/fractional cannabis industry consultant
  - Business name: Freed Solutions
  - Old email: freedsolutions@gmail.com (still active, forwarding)
  - New email: adam@freedsolutions.com
  - I use Google Workspace, Calendly, Notion, Cowork, Outlook
    (client), LinkedIn — all already updated

WHAT I NEED:

  1. A comprehensive checklist of every CATEGORY of account or
     asset that a freelance consultant in my position is likely
     to have tied to a Gmail address. Organized by priority
     (highest risk if missed → lowest).

  2. For each category, tell me:
     a) How to find accounts in that category tied to the old email
        (e.g. search Gmail for 'welcome' or 'subscription')
     b) How to update the email (account settings vs.
        create-new-and-migrate)
     c) Any gotchas or irreversible steps to watch for

  3. A method for doing a systematic Gmail inbox sweep to surface
     accounts I might have forgotten — specific search strings
     to run that will catch the long tail.

KNOWN CATEGORIES (already handled — skip these):
  Google Workspace, Calendly, Notion, Cowork, LinkedIn,
  resume files, landing page website

LIKELY CATEGORIES TO COVER (expand on each):
  - Software/SaaS tools (project mgmt, design, communication)
  - Domain registrar account (just created)
  - Social media profiles beyond LinkedIn
  - Financial/invoicing tools (Wave, QuickBooks, Stripe, etc.)
  - Banking or payment accounts tied to business
  - Industry directories or cannabis-specific platforms
  - Newsletter or content subscriptions
  - Two-factor authentication (2FA) recovery emails
  - Apple ID / Microsoft account if applicable
  - Any API keys or developer accounts (Google Cloud, etc.)
    — especially relevant given my automation work

After the audit, help me create a simple tracking spreadsheet
(Google Sheets format) to check off each item as I migrate it.
```

---

## What this phase covers

1. Comprehensive category checklist for freelance consultants
2. Gmail search strings to surface forgotten accounts
3. Per-category update instructions and gotcha warnings
4. 2FA recovery email audit (critical — easy to miss)
5. Google Cloud / API key accounts (relevant given automation work)
6. Migration tracking spreadsheet

---

> ✅ **Done when:** Every account category has been reviewed, updated or consciously deferred, and your tracking sheet is complete.
